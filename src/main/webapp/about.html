Pathways through History

About

Pathways through History describes a day at the Queensland Museum & Sciencecentre: a journey through time illustrating the exhibitions depicting life in Australia throughout the decades.

You will be taken back, a day at a time, to the number of people congregating around the eight cameras installed at the museum as we attempt to recreate their path around the exhibits.

To liven up the experience, we provide a snapshot of what the weather would have been like on these particular days.

Welcome to your pathway through history and let the adventure begin!

Resources

Data Sets

This project makes use of three data sets:

  • Queensland Museum People Counter

    Queensland Museum People Counter data (raw logs from their various camera-based counters).

  • Bureau of Meteorology Daily Weather Observations

    Provides weather observations for each day of the month. We used the minimum and maximum temperature, the number of hours of sunshine available and the amount of rainfall.

  • Queensland Museum South Bank (Past Exhibitions)

    Description

    Website

    The opening page of the Pathways through history website takes you back to some of the most successful exhibitions at the Queensland Museum & Science centre.

    Click on one of the icons to relive the moment when hundreds of people visited these truly wonderful events. You will explore an aerial view of levels 1 and 2 of the museum and dicover the hotspots of people congregating around the eight cameras throughout the museum.

    To liven up the experience we have linked the people's journey through the museum with daily weather observations from the Bureau of Meteorology and provided a snapshot of what the weather was likely to have been like for the visitors to the museum.

    A calendar icon, on the top right, depicts the day of interest and slightly below, icons illustrate the weather condition (sunny, cloudy, rainy) together with minimum and maximum temperatures of the day of interest.

    A slider at the top offers the user the opportunity to adjust the speed of execution to crawling tortoise speed or rabbit lightning fast.

    Application Programming Interface (API)

    The People Mover API makes use of a Representational State Transfer (REST) model to extract the information from the online resources and to provide access to a fixed set of operations. The REST resources and Data Model are accessible from here.

    Project Goals

    The overarching goal of the project was to provide a visual depiction of the journey of visitors to the Queensland Museum & Science centre. We also wanted to link this activity with a snapshot of the weather on that day and provide some context in the form of running exhibitions at the time of the visits.

    To achieve this goal, we have data mined the camera log files provided by the museum and extracted the timestamp and the in and out counter for each of the cameras.

    Next we have retrieved the Daily Weather Observations files organised by month, from the Bureau of Meteorology website. From these, we have extracted the daily minimum and maximum temperatures and the number of hours of sunshine during the day to reenact what the weather would have been like on that day.
